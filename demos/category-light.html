Computer Microphones

Start by prioritizing what is non-negotiable. Every user should demand a high score on the Voice Clarity slider; this is the fundamental purpose of the product. Next, evaluate your environment: if you have a noisy keyboard, a nearby fan, or other people in the room, the Background Noise Isolation slider becomes your most important feature.

Don't overpay for features you'll never use. Many premium mics include multi-pattern recording — great for ASMR or music, but overkill for Zoom calls. Watch out for mics marketed as "studio quality" that lack basic USB connectivity; you'll need extra interfaces.

Cardioid — Picks up sound from the front, rejects background noise. Condenser — More sensitive; captures detail but also ambient noise. Bit Depth/Sample Rate — 24-bit/96kHz is studio quality, 16-bit/48kHz is fine for voice. SPL — Max sound pressure before distortion.
